What are Indicators?

On any given day, stock prices may bounce around without apparent rhyme or reason. Experts call that market noise. Ordinary price and performance charts capture this noise with sharply jagged lines and frequent up-and-down movements. Often, there is so much action in the chart that it can be hard to see whether a stock is tracking to gain or lose value over time. In other words, the noise can drown out what the market may be trying to say about the true worth of an investment.

That's where stock market indicators can become useful. Indicators can help filter out the day-to-day noise so you can see direction more clearly and maybe even anticipate turning points in that direction. Singapore Exchange (SGX) offers you three indicators that are designed to help investors gain greater market clarity. They are the Simple Moving Average indicator, the Moving Average Convergence Divergence indicator, and the Relative Strength Index. Each indicator alone may tell an important story. In combination, they can help support a market hypothesis or help highlight hidden shortcomings in it.
